## Course Overview

This is an open, discussion-driven session in which we examine good and bad practices in AnyLogic simulation modeling. Through real-world examples, participants will learn to identify common pitfalls, understand their consequences, and adopt patterns that lead to more maintainable, scalable, and reliable simulation models.

---

## Format

Unlike structured lecture courses, this session is built around examples and discussion. Participants are encouraged to bring their own models and experiences to discuss.

---

## Topics Explored

- Code organization and readability
- Naming conventions and documentation
- Model structure and modularity
- Performance pitfalls and their fixes
- Common anti-patterns in AnyLogic projects
- Transforming messy models into clean, maintainable ones
- When and how to refactor simulation models

---

## Who Should Attend

This course is ideal for practitioners who already have working experience with AnyLogic and want to elevate the quality and professionalism of their simulation work. Whether you're building models solo or as part of a team, this session will help you avoid costly mistakes and adopt industry best practices.
